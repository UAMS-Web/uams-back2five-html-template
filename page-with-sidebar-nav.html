Text Styles Examples

There are a few simple rules to follow when using headings:

Every page should have an h1 heading

Without an h1 the user has no simple way to learn what the page is about. In most themes, the $title variable is wrapped in h1 tags. The $title variable outputs the main title for the page. For example, on this page, the title is “Accessibility”.

Some pages — for example, the default theme front page — do not have a title. On the front page, it is an accepted practice to wrap the site name in the h1 tag.

Headings must be properly nested

Heading levels must not be skipped. For example, do not jump directly from h1 to h3.

Headings are for structure, not formatting

Headings should be used to divide content into meaningful sections, not to format text. Your h2 styling may be red, but that does not mean that anytime you want red text you should make the text an h2. Rather, the headings (no matter what their styling looks like) should be used to designate content structure.

Blockquote

The blockquote is used to indicate the quotation of a large section of text from another source. It can be as long or as short as you’d like!

Those of us that had been up all night were in no mood for coffee and donuts, we wanted strong drink. We were, after all, the absolute cream of the national sporting press.
